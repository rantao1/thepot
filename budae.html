Budae Jjigae

Introducing the yummiest and historical Korean food

The story of the origins of budae jjigae (also spelled boodae chigae) that circulates in Korean American communities begins during the Korean War, when most Koreans were starving. Word spread that American soldiers stationed there had an endless supply of food, with portions so big that they could afford to throw food away. The bases became destinations for hungry Koreans, who scavenged or purchased the remnants. As some survivors recalled, this food was not exactly palatable; it was often a mélange of various food scraps mixed with inedible things, such as cigarette butts.

Those starving people during Korean War recalled that though the food was sometimes disgusting, it kept them alive. Some people would sort through the scraps and find a perfectly intact pink slab of jellied ham and put it in a stew. What the Americans didn’t finish, Koreans used to make the first iterations of budae jjigae..

Army stew is still popular in South Korea. It's a trendy table-cooking food in restaurants and often eaten while drinking soju (rice liquor). Budae Jjiage has evolved since its post-war origins. It now has more Korean ingredients such as ddeok (rice cakes), kimchi, gochujang and various types of noodles.

The paste for the soup can be different for different regions or individuals but usually it contains Korean special Gochu-jang, soy sauce, and sesame oil.

There are a plenty of options for soup based on personal favors such as rice water, anchovy soup and beef bone soup. In Korea, the easiest and the most popular soup base for most of stew dishes is 'rice water'. Don't throw the water away that comes from washing rice, it will make your soup very deep and flavor.

SPAM, American canned cooked meat is a must-have-ingredient for Budae jjigae. This is makes the flavor of this dish so special and also you'll be able to taste the history of Korean War!

Sausage is another special meat for this dish which makes Budae jjigae a special Korean fushion and historical food. This is also a must-have-ingredient.

You must not forget Kimchi to make Budade jjigae perfect of course, because you know, Kimchi is the most traditional Korean food ingredient. There are various types of Kimchi, but for this dish, you should use cabbage version.

Tofu, also known as bean curd, is a very popular ingredient in East Asian cuisines. It will give you a little bit of healtiness. Tofu can be soft, firm, or etra firm. But I recommend you use either firm tofu or extra firm tofu, which are usually used for stew dishes.

Rice cake is not a must-have-item but will make the dish rich. The texture of rice cake can be fun, it's very soft and chewy, it became very famous ingredient for Koreans these days.

For the authentic version of Budae jjigae, baked beans are traditionally used. But, these days, there are many Koreans who prefer modern version to traditional version and they generally skip using baked beans. It's up to your preference. You can try both two versions!

Instant noodle is becoming a very popular ingredient for Budae jjigae. Even though there are many different kinds of recipes for Budae jjigae, in Korea, most of Koreans prefer to put instant noodle. If you have one, then you're ready to make modern version of Budae jjigae.

Once you place all of your preferred ingredients into a pot, it's time to put sauce and pour soup in it. The next step would be...

After 5-7mins boiling, put instant ramen in to the pot. Then, once it gets done, the dish is done! Enjoy!
