Much like the Shabu-Shabu in Japan and fondue in Western cooking, Chinese hot pot, a.k.a. huo guo (火锅), is a group dining activity around the table with a pot of boiling broth in the middle over a gas stove. Friends and families cook the raw ingredients of their liking in this communal pot of broth while chitchat, laugh, and enjoy each other’s company by the tableside.

As the name suggested, Sichuan hot pot was originated from Sichuan Province which is known for its appetite for spicy food. This mouth-burning and tongue-numbing hotpot has been gaining enormous amount of attention in recent years in United States.

The commonly accepted theory traces the Sichuan Hot Pot back to early 20 century. At the time, the port workers and fishermen worked and lived along the Yangtze River could not afford to eat the regular cuts of meat so they figured out a way to cook cheap meats and offal by boiling them in a pot of extremely spicy broth. The use of spicy broth was first intended to disguise the faulty taste from the offal. However after some modification and addition of fragrant spices, this one pot meal became a popular dish in Sichuan region.

After years of continuous development, the Sichuan hotpot today is much more refined and no longer a meal for the lower-class. Although offal still remains as classic hotpot ingredients, high quality meats and seafood ingredients are also common on the menu. The modern hot pot has been shape into a progressive meal that starts with sliced meat, offal, and seafood followed by vegetables and ends with noodles.

The Half & Half hotpot, a.k.a. the Yuan-Yang (鸳鸯) hotpot, is one of the most commonly used pots. It was said that when this pot was invented, the middle plate was engraved with Yuan-Yang, which is also called the Mandarin Duck. Another story about the name is that the Mandarin Duck always appears in couples, which vests the pot with romantic meaning.

The aged spicy paste is the soul of Sichuan hot pot. Following the secret recipe, different spices are mixed together and cooked in low heat for around 30 minutes to burst the best flavors.

There are a plenty of options for soup based on personal favors such as water, fish soup and beef soup. In Sichuan, the most authentic choice is beef tallow which will increase the level of spiciness as the time of boiling goes on.

Thin cut meat is a very popular ingredient dish. The meat can be lamb, beef, and pork. As each piece is very thin, it is easy to cook and absorb the flavor of the soup easily.

Meatball is another way to cook meat in the hot pot. It makes the meat juicy with the attractive flavor on the surface.

Lotus can be cooked relatively longer which can absorb thick flavors and stay crispy. It gives a unique taste and texture for hot pot.

Napa cabbage is a traditional Chinese vegetable. It is usually cooked in the mild soup as the leaves are very easy to absorb flavor and become very spicy.

Seafood such as fish and shrimp are usually served in the form of meatball. It is easy to cook and eat for a hot pot party.

Cold dishes are served to help cool your mouth. They are usually fresh vegetables which will give you a fresh and juicy break.

Duck tongue is another typical and authentic dish for hotpot. It is tender and gives people a unique taste with crispy soft bone in hot pot.

Each hot pot is filled with a steaming aromatic broth that is decorated with an assortment of fresh ingredients.
The aged spicy paste is the soul of Sichuan hot pot and is usually guarded by restaurant owners as top-secret.

The magical and fascinated flavor of hot pot is also attributed by the fresh ingredients.

Eating hot pot is a slow and interactive process and people always engage in a conversation while waiting for the food to be ready.

Hot pot meats are cut very thin, so they only take a few seconds to cook through. You will have to dip them in multiple times, but it only takes about a minute total to get fully cooked meat. Here is a quick demonstrationon how to hot pot a piece of raw beef.

  • Pick up the beef with your chopsticks
  • Submerge it in the boiling broth for about 30 seconds
  • Remove from the broth and eat with your dipping sauce

Before you start feasting on your hot pot feast, you can't forget about the dipping sauces. Just like the broth, there are many options.

In the Chinese culture, hot pot represents an ideology of Yuan-Fen (缘分), a.k.a. destiny, which is a concept in the Chinese folk religion describing good and bad chances and potential relationships.
Today, hot pot is a very popular type of meal for the reunion of family or friends. Just like the reunion of Sichuan pepper and red chili, Yuan-Fen brought people together and created the magical and delicious 'flavor' in their lifes. Meeting with old friends? Have hot pot together!
